# Inspirations

Central reference of source material and what's borrowed from each.

## RAGE (id Software, 2011/2019)

- **Weapon design**: Unique guns with distinct alt-fire modes (e.g., shotgun slug, rocket manual detonate). Each weapon feels different rather than tiered stat bumps.
- **Overdrive**: Temporary combat steroid — increased damage, speed, health regen. Built through combat, activated manually. Frequent-enough to use every encounter but short enough to require timing.
- **Post-apocalyptic open world**: Wasteland with scattered settlements, enemy factions, and hidden engineering schematics.
- **Engineering system**: Find schematics in the world to unlock weapon upgrades and gadgets. Progression through discovery, not XP bars.
- **Wingstick**: Boomerang projectile that bounces between targets — a signature weapon worth creating a BattleOx equivalent.

## Mass Effect (BioWare)

- **Primer/detonator combos**: Apply a status (lifted, frozen, burning, stunned) with one ability, trigger it with another for bonus effects. Creates synergy within loadouts.
- **Sci-fi power fantasy**: Flashy physics-driven powers, not just damage numbers.
- **Squad dynamics**: Teammate ability synergy (future consideration for co-op).
- **Weapon feel**: Satisfying feedback, distinct audio/visual identity per gun.

## Overwatch (Blizzard)

- **Ability loadout system**: 3 cooldown-based ability slots (movement/utility, combat/control, ultimate) assigned to hotkeys. Players build custom kits.
- **Role composition** (TBD): Tank / Damage / Support roles for team-based modes — broader ability kits with role-specific passives.
- **Ultimate economy**: Charge builds through combat activity. High-impact, long-cooldown. Strategic timing matters more than spam.
- **Ping/communication system (TBD)**: Contextual radial menu — mark enemy, location, warning, request. Reduces voice chat dependency.
- **Scoreboard (TBD)**: Tab view showing kills, damage, healing, objective time per player.

## Fortnite (Epic Games)

- **Resource harvesting**: Swing tool at world objects → gain materials. Fast, tactile, satisfying.
- **Combat building (TBD)**: Quick-place walls, ramps, floors during fights. Skill-gap mechanic. Zero-build mode for casual play.
- **Battle Royale (TBD)**: Last-standing loop — drop, loot, storm shrinks, fight. 1-4 player squads.
- **Party/social flow**: Invite friends, squad up, ready-up. Minimal friction between social and play.

## Minecraft (Mojang)

- **Creative building (TBD)**: Free-form block grid for bases, defenses, art. Permanent, persistent.
- **Exploration-driven progression**: Venture out to find resources → build tools → venture further. Natural difficulty curve.
- **Day/night preparation rhythm**: Spend daylight gathering/building, survive the night under attack. Existing system aligns perfectly.
- **Material variety**: Different block types, ores, crafting materials with distinct purposes.

## Roblox (Roblox Corp)

- **Tool system**: Universal item framework — every equippable is a **Tool** with:
  - **Handle**: 3D mesh visible in-world
  - **Grip**: Position/rotation offset that places it in the character's hand
  - **Activation**: Click to use (swing, fire, throw, place, etc.)
  - **Properties**: Damage, cooldown, ammo stored on the Tool itself
  - Bow, sword, and future weapons all become Tool instances rather than hardcoded scripts.
- **Player customization (TBD)**: Avatar skins, accessories, emotes purchased or earned.
- **Social sandbox**: Drop-in/drop-out multiplayer, user-created experiences, low barrier to play with friends.

## 7 Days to Die (The Fun Pimps)

- **Horde night defense**: Nightly or periodic zombie horde that pathfinds to the player's base and breaks through blocks. Gives building a purpose — not aesthetic, *survival*.
- **Voxel-based building**: Every block is harvestable, placeable, destructible. Bases get damaged in horde nights, forcing repair and improvement.
- **Loot progression via schematics**: Find schematics for better tools/weapons. No skill points — progression is "can I craft this yet?"
- **Progression-light framing**: Take the horde defense loop and schematic discovery. Skip the skill point perk trees and XP leveling.

## Valheim (Iron Gate Studio)

- **Biome identity**: Each biome (Meadows, Black Forest, Swamp, Mountain, Plains) feels fundamentally different — distinct color palettes, enemies, resources, and danger level. Not just noise parameters.
- **Structural integrity building**: Supporting beams matter. Builds must be structurally sound. Creates meaningful design decisions.
- **Co-op survival loop**: Proven multiplayer sandbox that sustains hundreds of hours through exploration + building + boss fights.
- **Progression-light framing**: Take biome *feel* (not boss gates) and structural building depth. Skip the gated progression — player goes anywhere, difficulty is zone-based and subtle.

## No Man's Sky (Hello Games)

- **Procedural diversity at scale**: Flora, fauna, color palettes, weather systems differ between planets. For BattleOx: biome variety should feel genuinely distinct, not just parameter tweaks.
- **Base building with purpose**: Build outposts for resource extraction, teleporters, storage. Base serves the exploration loop (go further → build forward base → go further still).
- **Multi-tool system**: One tool swaps between mining, combat, scanning, terrain manipulation. Maps well onto the Roblox Tool system — one item, multiple modes.
- **Progression-light framing**: Take procedural variety and purposeful base placement. Skip the nanite/unit economy complexity.

## The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild / Tears of the Kingdom (Nintendo) — Long-Term

- **Physics sandbox**: Every object has weight, buoyancy, and physics response. TotK's Ultrahand, Fuse, Ascend, and Recall let players *build solutions* rather than follow prescribed paths. This is the north star for the sandbox pillar.
- **Glider traversal**: Paraglider transforms how the player reads terrain — every high point becomes a launch pad.
- **Emergent problem solving**: Players solve encounters their own way (build a bridge, set grass on fire, roll a boulder downhill). No one "correct" path.
- **Progression-light framing**: Take the physics-emergent design philosophy. Weapon durability is noted but not adopted (inventory management overhead conflicts with the action focus).

## Titanfall 2 (Respawn Entertainment)

- **Movement system**: Wall run, slide, double jump, grapple hook. Best-in-class FPS mobility. Every surface is a potential path.
- **Progression-light framing**: Take the movement *feel* — evaluate wall run, slide, and grapple as additions to the existing dash roll and charge jump.

## Doom Eternal (id Software)

- **Aggressive combat loop**: Fight to resupply. Glory kills drop health, flame belch drops armor, chainsaw drops ammo. The correct play is always *toward* enemies, not away.
- **Progression-light framing**: Take the push-forward loop philosophy. Formalize Overdrive with this mentality — combat rewards aggression. Skip the suit upgrade point system.

## Halo (Bungie / 343 Industries)

- **Weapon sandbox design**: Every weapon has a unique role with zero overlap. A pistol is not a weaker battle rifle — it fills a different niche. This is the guiding philosophy for the RAGE weapon overhaul.
- **Equipment system**: Deployable items (grapple, drop wall, thruster, shield) as single-use pickups — a lighter alternative to cooldown-based abilities.
- **Vehicle combat**: Future consideration for larger-scale modes.
- **Progression-light framing**: Take the weapon sandbox philosophy and equipment items. No unlock grind — guns are on the map.
